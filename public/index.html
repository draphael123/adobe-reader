Why Windows Shows a Warning

Windows SmartScreen warns about downloads from new or independent developers even if the app is completely safe. This happens because:

The Capture Engine

Here's where the magic happens. Every time you flip to a new page, we grab a screenshot. But we're smart about it no duplicates, no wasted space.

  • 1

    Page Change Detection

    We monitor the visible content. When it changes significantly (new page), we trigger a capture. Simple scroll doesn't count.

  • 2

    Perceptual Hashing (AI Magic)

    Before saving, we generate a "fingerprint" of the image. If it matches a previous capture too closely, we skip it. Your mouse cursor moving won't fool us.

  • 3

    Smart File Naming

    Files are named with timestamps and page numbers: Page_001_14-32-15.png

Duplicate threshold: Adjustable from 80% to 100% in settings. Lower = stricter (fewer duplicates saved). Default 90% works great for most PDFs.

Make It Yours

Control freaks, rejoice! Adjust capture delays, choose image formats, set quality levels, and more. It's your tool make it work your way.

  • Output Location

    Choose where screenshots land. Default is Documents/PDF Screenshots, but anywhere works. Network drives too!

  • Image Format & Quality

    PNG (lossless), JPEG (smaller), or WebP (best of both). Quality slider from 60-100%. Balance size vs. clarity.

  • Capture Timing

    Set delay after page changes (100-2000ms). Gives slow PDFs time to render before capture. Default 300ms is solid.

Privacy Policy

TL;DR: We Don't Collect Anything

Seriously. This app runs entirely on your computer. We have no servers. We don't see your screenshots. We don't know you exist. It's actually a bit lonely for us.

The Longer Version (for lawyers)

The only network request is an optional check for updates, which pings GitHub's API. You can disable this. We don't track you, we don't analyze your usage, we don't sell your data (because we don't have any).

Your Screenshots

Stored locally on YOUR computer, in a folder YOU choose. We couldn't access them even if we wanted to (which we don't).
